About Safety Research in Texas

Texas is the second-largest state by both area and population, encompassing wildly different regional economies and safety landscapes. The state's major metros — Houston, Dallas, San Antonio, and Austin — each have distinct crime patterns shaped by rapid suburban growth, while smaller cities and rural areas tend to show different trends entirely. Texas's lack of statewide zoning regulation has led to some of the most sprawling metro areas in the country, which often correlates with wide safety score variation even between neighboring ZIP codes.

Because Texas cities have grown so quickly over the past two decades, many of the newest suburban ZIP codes on the edges of Houston, Dallas, and Austin tend to show stronger safety profiles than older urban cores. When researching a move within Texas, it's worth comparing several nearby ZIP codes rather than relying on city-wide reputation alone.
