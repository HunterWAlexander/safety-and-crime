About Safety Research in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania's safety landscape is shaped largely by its two major metros, Philadelphia and Pittsburgh, which sit at opposite ends of the state and have very different economic histories. Philadelphia's dense historic neighborhoods see typical big-city crime patterns near the urban core, while Pittsburgh's more compact geography and economic revitalization over recent decades have shaped a somewhat different safety profile.

Smaller Pennsylvania cities like Allentown also factor into the state's overall safety picture, often showing crime patterns more in line with mid-sized industrial cities elsewhere in the Northeast and Midwest. As with most states, suburban ZIP codes surrounding Philadelphia and Pittsburgh tend to show more favorable safety scores than the urban cores.
