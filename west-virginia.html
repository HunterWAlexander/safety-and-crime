About Safety Research in West Virginia

West Virginia's predominantly rural and mountainous geography has shaped a safety landscape concentrated around Charleston, the state capital, and Huntington along the Ohio River. Both cities reflect West Virginia's historic ties to coal and manufacturing industries, with safety patterns shaped by decades of economic transition.

West Virginia's low overall population density and strong Appalachian community character contribute to generally favorable statewide safety statistics, even as the state continues to navigate economic challenges tied to its historic industrial base.
