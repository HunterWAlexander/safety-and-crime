About Safety in Los Angeles

Los Angeles is the second-largest city in the U.S. and spans an enormous geographic footprint that includes everything from the affluent enclaves of Beverly Hills and Westwood to densely populated urban corridors in South LA. This sheer size means safety profiles can shift dramatically within just a few miles, making ZIP-code-level research especially important for anyone considering a move to the LA metro area.

Like many major West Coast cities, LA has experienced fluctuating crime trends over the past decade, with property crime remaining a persistent concern in certain commercial and tourist-heavy areas. Suburban-style neighborhoods within city limits, particularly in the western and northern parts of LA, tend to show stronger safety scores than the urban core.
