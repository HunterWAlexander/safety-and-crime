About Safety Research in Ohio

Ohio's safety landscape is shaped by its three major metros — Columbus, Cleveland, and Cincinnati — each representing different economic histories within the broader Rust Belt region. Columbus has seen significant growth and revitalization in recent years, often correlating with improving safety trends in certain neighborhoods, while parts of Cleveland and Cincinnati still reflect longer-term economic challenges tied to deindustrialization.

As with many Midwestern states, Ohio's suburban communities surrounding its major cities tend to show meaningfully different safety profiles than the urban cores. Anyone researching a move within Ohio should pay close attention to the specific ZIP code rather than relying on city-wide statistics alone.
