About Safety Research in Wyoming

Wyoming, the least populous U.S. state, has a safety landscape concentrated around Cheyenne, the state capital, and Casper, both of which reflect the state's historic ties to energy and ranching industries. Wyoming's vast open spaces and extremely low population density mean most of the state shows safety patterns typical of small rural communities.

Wyoming's proximity to Yellowstone and other major tourist destinations creates seasonal variation in some communities, though the state overall generally shows favorable safety statistics consistent with its low population density and strong rural character.
