About Safety in Chicago

Chicago is the third-largest city in the United States, known for its distinct neighborhoods that each carry their own character and safety profile. The Loop and surrounding downtown ZIP codes see heavy commercial foot traffic which can elevate property crime statistics, while residential neighborhoods like Lincoln Park and Lakeview tend to show meaningfully stronger safety scores due to lower density and different housing patterns.

Chicago's crime statistics have historically shown significant variation between the North Side, South Side, and West Side of the city, reflecting decades of differing economic investment and demographic patterns. Anyone researching a move to Chicago should pay close attention to specific ZIP codes rather than relying on city-wide averages, given how much variation exists within city limits.
