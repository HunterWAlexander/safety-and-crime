About Safety Research in Florida

Florida's population has grown rapidly over the past decade, particularly in metro areas like Orlando, Tampa, and the broader Miami region. Tourist-heavy areas near beaches and entertainment districts often see elevated property crime statistics due to high foot traffic, while residential suburbs further from tourist zones tend to show stronger safety profiles. The state's year-round warm climate and lack of state income tax have driven significant migration, reshaping the safety landscape of many ZIP codes.

Florida's geography spans everything from dense urban cores in Miami to quiet retirement communities and rapidly developing suburbs around Orlando and Tampa, meaning safety scores can vary considerably even within the same county. Newer planned communities, especially in Central Florida, frequently show some of the more favorable safety statistics in the state.
