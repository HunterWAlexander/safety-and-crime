About Safety Research in New Jersey

New Jersey's safety landscape is heavily shaped by its proximity to both New York City and Philadelphia, with significant population density concentrated in northern cities like Newark and Jersey City. These urban centers show typical big-city crime patterns, while many of the state's suburban communities benefit from proximity to major metro job markets.

Trenton, the state capital, shows a distinct safety profile shaped by government employment and historic industrial decline. New Jersey's extremely high population density relative to its small size means safety can vary significantly within short distances.
