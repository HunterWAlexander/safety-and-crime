About Safety in Houston

Houston is the fourth-largest city in the United States and the largest in Texas, spanning a massive metro area that includes dense urban neighborhoods, sprawling suburbs, and everything in between. This geographic diversity means safety varies dramatically by ZIP code — areas like River Oaks and Memorial tend to show stronger safety profiles due to lower population density and higher median incomes, while ZIP codes closer to the urban core like Downtown and Third Ward typically see higher crime rates consistent with most major U.S. city centers.

Houston's rapid growth and lack of zoning regulations have created a uniquely sprawling urban landscape compared to other major U.S. cities, which contributes to wide variation in safety scores even between neighboring ZIP codes. When researching a move to Houston, it's worth looking beyond just the city-wide reputation and drilling into the specific ZIP code or neighborhood you're considering, since the safety experience in Energy Corridor can look very different from Downtown despite both being within city limits.
